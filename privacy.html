When we refer in this policy to 'you', we are referring to a customer of our services, or a person visiting our website. We refer to those people in this policy as 'Subscriber'. We do not have any relationship with Subscribers, and process information relating to them solely for the purposes of providing our service to our customers.

When we refer to a 'Subscribers list' in this policy, we are referring to details of Subscribers (including their email addresses) processed by us on your behalf to provide you with our MailGen service.

If you are a Subscriber and wish to cease receiving emails from one of our customers, please unsubscribe directly using the unsubscribe link in the customer's email, or contact the customer directly.

If a Subscriber makes a direct request to be removed from the Subscribers list of one of our customers, we may do so on behalf of our customer, while providing notice to the customer of the Subscriber's request. Our customer is the data controller in respect of Subscriber' personal data, and Subscribers should consult our customer's own Privacy Policy for details on the customer's data protection practices. We will never use the Subscriber email addresses to send our own informational and promotional content. We may refer to Subscriber's personal data when generating usage reports and analysis as data processor for our data controller customers. Your subscriber lists

Your subscriber lists are stored on secure servers. They will also be available to our email service providers ("ESPs"). ESPs will only have access to your lists when you are sending an email. Once the email is sent, the ESPs no longer have access to your subscribers list. Our ESPs are Mailgun, SendGrid, Sparkpost and Elastic Email. We don't, under any circumstances, sell or share your contact lists with anyone else. If someone on your subscriber list complains or contacts us, only then will we respond to that person. Only you, our authorised employees, and our ESPs have access to view your subscriber lists.

We may also monitor those events for the purposes of administering our service (including checking for any abuse of our service) and research on patterns and trends in the use of our service. We will never use any Subscriber data for the purposes of that administration or generating that research. It will always be conducted on an aggregated and anonymised dataset, which does not identify any individual Subscriber.

You may export (download) your subscriber lists from MailGen at any time. We'll only ever use and disclose the information in your Subscriber lists for the reasons listed in this section or in the section entitled 'How we use your personal information' above.

We will never use or disclose the information in your subscriber lists to send our own informational and promotional content. If we detect abusive or illegal behaviour related to your subscriber list, we may share your subscriber list or portions of it with affected internet service providers (“ISPs") or anti-spam organisations. We may also be required to disclose it to law enforcement or regulatory bodies. We will only do so if legally required.

We may conduct analysis on your use of the service and the results generated by your emails sent by means of the service. This analysis is conducted solely on an aggregated and anonymised basis.

The periods for which we keep your information depend on why your information was collected and what we use it for. We will not keep your personal information for longer than necessary for our business purposes or for legal requirements.

For your convenience we will keep your account open (and therefore retain your data so that it is available to you) for up to 13 months after your last sign in or use of our service. Prior to closing your account and deleting your data, we will attempt to send warning emails to your account email address so that you have an opportunity to keep your account open or back up any data that you need. We may contact you about our services during this time, unless you have asked us not to contact you.

At any point you may delete your account and the data you have provided via the Dashboard. Copies of your data may remain for up to 90 days in backup storage, which we use to recover from an unexpected event that results in data loss. We may also keep limited information for up to 6 years for compliance and accounting purposes and to enforce or defend any legal claims in respect of our terms of service.

Your rights

You have the right to request access to personal data that we may process about you.

You have the right to require us to correct any inaccuracies in your data, free of charge. If you wish to exercise this right, you should:

  1. 1. Put your request in an email to us
  2. 2. Provide us with enough information to identify you (e.g. username or email address)
  3. 3. Specify the information that is incorrect and what it should be replaced with

You can access, correct, update or request deletion of your personal information at any time, either through your online account or by contacting us.

Deletion of data will be carried out on the understanding that removal of some information (e.g. email address) during an active membership term may negatively affect your ability to use the MailGen service.

We cannot delete any invoices, as these are kept for tax purposes.

You can request that we restrict processing of your personal information, object to processing of your information or request portability of your personal information. For these requests please contact us. We will comply with your request where your rights have been exercised in accordance with applicable laws.

If we have collected and processed your personal information with your consent, then you can withdraw that consent at any time. To be clear, we may still continue to process your data if we have a different legal basis for doing so (for example, if we are required by law to do so, or we need to do so for the purposes of fulfilling our obligations to you under our terms and conditions of service).

You also have the right to ask us to stop processing your personal data for direct marketing purposes. You can do this through your MailGen dashboard or via email. If you wish to exercise this right via email, you should:

  1. 1. Put your request in writing (an email with a header that says 'Unsubscribe' is acceptable)
  2. 2. Provide us with enough information to identify you (e.g. email address)
  3. 2. If your objection is not to direct marketing in general, but to direct marketing by a particular channel (e.g. email or telephone), please specify the channel you are objecting to.
